I love motorcycles and how they are able to balance on two wheels while in motion. There are many aspect of this phenomenom why the bike doesn't fall over, but one that I find interesting the is gyroscopic force created by the spinning wheels. When you take a corner and lean, you feel different forces on your body as you carry speed. That force is called the centripital force and involves the inward force you feel the motorcycle puting on your body as you follow the path of a circle. Riding a motorcycle is thrilling because of a sense of freedom you get from not being enclosed like you are in a car or truck. Sure there are dangers, but if you take a safety training course and learn to read the drivers around you, riding motorcycles can be safe, efficient, and adventurous.
